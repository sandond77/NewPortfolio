About Me

Hi, I’m Sandon (pronounced like Brandon except with an S). I graduated from UC Davis in 2016 with a degree in Biological Systems Engineering. I currently work at a biotech company called Grifols, where I manufacture proteins used in blood testing kits for the detection of viral diseases like HIV and Hepatitis. I recently finished a full-stack coding bootcamp from UC Berkeley (April 2018) and am currently making a transition to be becoming a full-time coder/full-stack developer.

I’ve been around computers my entire life and got into computer gaming at an early age. At the age of 4, I was already sitting in front of a Windows 98 Compaq desktop playing games such as Roller Coaster Tycoon and Starcraft. I got my first taste of coding in college through my engineering courses. I had to learn MATLAB - a math computation language. I really struggled to learn it, but it helped me realize that I enjoyed coding. Learning MATLAB provided me with a good foundation that would help me excel at the UC Berkeley Coding Bootcamp. I was never very creative or artistic growing up - terrible at drawing and not musically inclined at all. Coding and designing an application, to me, requires a lot of creativity and thinking. There’s a satisfaction that comes with watching your code work after spending long hours on it.
